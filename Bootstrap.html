buttery pav bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular, quick Indian street food from Maharashtra, featuring a spicy, mashed vegetable curry (bhaji) made with potatoes, tomatoes, peas, and bell peppers, cooked with spices and served with buttered, toasted bread rolls (pav). Originating in the 1850s for textile workers, it's known for its rich flavor, soft buns, and vibrant presentation with garnishes of butter, chopped onions, and lemon, offering a satisfying vegetarian meal or snack.

crispy dosa

Dosa is a popular, thin, savory South Indian crepe made from a fermented batter of rice and black gram (urad dal), known for its crispy texture and served with accompaniments like sambar (lentil stew) and coconut chutney, often stuffed with spiced potatoes (masala dosa) or enjoyed plain (sada dosa). This nutritious, gluten-free dish is enjoyed globally for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, with variations arising from ingredients like semolina (rava) or different cooking methods.

Rice Manchurianis a popular, flavorful Indo-Chinese fusion dish featuring crispy, deep-fried vegetable balls (or sometimes paneer/chicken) tossed in a spicy, tangy Manchurian sauce, served with or mixed into wok-tossed fried rice, creating a balance of textures and bold tastes with ingredients like soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and bell peppers.
